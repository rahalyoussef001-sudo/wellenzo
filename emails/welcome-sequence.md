Hey,

How many times have you told yourself: *"I just need to try harder tomorrow"* or *"I just need more discipline"*?

Here is the truth: for an ADHD brain, willpower is a biological lie. 

Standard productivity advice tells you to "just start." But their brains have normal dopamine receptor activity. When they look at a boring task, their prefrontal cortex (PFC) easily projects the future reward of finishing it, providing the motivation to begin.

For an ADHD brain, that chemical connection is weak. Your prefrontal cortex does not register the future reward. It only registers the immediate mental friction of starting. 

When that friction is high, your **amygdala**—the brain's threat-detector—registers the task as a stressor and shuts down your system. You physically freeze.

Trying to "will" your way through a freeze state is like trying to drive a car with a locked transmission. You will only burn out the engine.

These actions trigger a safe cortisol and dopamine release, priming your prefrontal cortex to initiate the deconstruction hack.
